**1. Project Overview:-**

This project focuses on analyzing employee data to help organizations monitor workforce trends, hiring activity, performance, and demographic distribution.
Using the HR dataset, an interactive dashboard was designed to provide deep insights into employee hiring, terminations, departmental distribution, gender balance, education, income comparisons, and geographical patterns.

________________________________________

**2. Dataset Description:-**

This dataset contains information about each employee, including:-

•	Personal details (name, age, gender)

•	Job details (department, job title, hire/termination date)

•	Salary and education

•	Location (city, state, HQ/branch)

•	Performance rating

________________________________________

**3. Business Objectives:-**

•	Monitor overall workforce strength by tracking total hired, active, and terminated employees.

•	 Compare employee distribution between Headquarters (New York) and branch locations for better resource management.

•	Understand geographic workforce distribution across cities and states to support location-based HR decisions.

•	Review demographic patterns such as gender, age, and education level to ensure diversity and balanced workforce representation.

•	Analyse income and salary variations by education, gender, job title, and age to support fair compensation practices.

•	Identify performance trends across different education levels to assist in training and development planning.

________________________________________

**4. Key Business Questions:-**

1.	How are our hiring and termination trends affecting total active employees?
2.	Which departments have the most employees or highest turnover?
3.	What is the gender distribution in the company?
4.	How does education level relate to employee performance?
5.	Are there salary differences by age, gender, or education?
6.	 Which locations have the highest employee count?
   
________________________________________

**5. Tools & Techniques:-**

•	Tableau Desktop

•	Data Cleaning & Transformation (removing duplicates, fixing data types)

•	Calculated Fields (Total Employees, Hired, Terminated, Age Group, Salary Band)

•	Parameters & Dynamic Filters (Gender, Location, Department, Hire Date)

•	 KPI Cards (Active Employees, Hires, Terminations)

•	Trend & Time-Series Charts (Hiring vs Termination over time)

•	Demographic Visuals (Gender Donut Chart, Age Distribution)

•	Heatmaps (Education Level vs Performance Rating)

________________________________________

**6. Insight:-**

•	The company has a steady increase in total employees, supported by consistent hiring trends.

•	IT and Finance departments show the highest employee count, indicating strong workforce demand in these areas.

•	Gender distribution is balanced, but some departments have slightly higher male representation.

•	Most employees fall in the 25–35 age group, showing a young workforce with growth potential.

•	Employees with Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees form the largest group, and they also show stronger performance ratings.

•	Salary increases with age and education, with higher pay observed for Master’s and PhD holders.

•	Locations like HQ and major branches have the highest employee concentration.

•	Terminations remain lower than hiring, showing stable employee retention.
